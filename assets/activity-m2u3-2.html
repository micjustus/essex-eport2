UML Practical Activity:

Object Diagram

Diagram Description

The UML diagram presented is my first attempt at representig a fictitious supermarket. The practical activity was designed to get students to consider UML conventions and types of relationships; to think in terms of objects and their relationships such as Association, Specialization, Composition and Aggregation relationships as well as the multiplicities (and names) of relationships.

Method to Develop the Diagram

In considering the objects of the fictitious supermarket, I relied on forming sentences to help identify all objects of the system. Then, I drafted initial diagrams and thereafter, based on further revisions of the diagrams, I produced a UML object model to observe the model at a single point in time. I found the approach works in tandem with my way of thinking about modelling for the practical activity, and helps me to understand the benefits of modelling object models first versus class diagrams.

Sentences used to identify objects:

  • Staff works with one or more Order Processing Devices.
  • An Order Processing Device is either a Mobile Shopper Scanner or a Till.
  • An Order Processing Device scans a Customer's Loyalty Card.
  • A Loyalty Card is part of a Loyalty Scheme.
  • A Loyalty Scheme is either Basic or provides Double Points.
  • A Supplier can provide one or more Products.
  • A Customer participates in zero or one Loyalty Schemes.
  • A Customer resides at a single Address.
  • A Customer will perform a checkout operation either by themself OR by interacting with a Staff member (who acts in a particular Role).
  • An Order Processing Device is used to process a Customer's Order which is an Order Basket.
  • An Order consists of one or more Products.

However, reviewing the model, I noted that the first draft does not quite follow my real-world experience. So modelling my experience of using Sainbury's (UK) as inspiration, resulted in a second revision shown below:

I like the second attempt a lot more than the first attempt, because of the introduction of the following:

  • Association Classes.

    Order is now an association class between Customer and Order Capture Device. This implies that an Order must exist when a Customer is associated with an Order Capture Device. Product List is also an association clause because the list of Product must be passed from a Mobile Shopper Scanner to a Self Checkout Till whenever a Customer wishes to conclude their Order using the self-checkout route.

  • Receipt.

    The model shows that a Receipt is generated by either a Self Checkout Till or a normal Till instance.

  • Order Capture Device.

    The model shows that a Customer can use three different methods to process their Order.
